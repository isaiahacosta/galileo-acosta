STORY/HISTORY

Manolo Fortich National High School (MFNHS), located in the municipality of Manolo Fortich in Bukidnon, Philippines, has played a significant role in the educational development of the region. The school was established as a response to the growing need for accessible secondary education in the area, which has a rich agricultural base and a steadily increasing population. Over the years, MFNHS has evolved to accommodate the changing educational demands, offering a variety of academic tracks and programs in line with the Philippine education system. With the implementation of the K-12 program, the school expanded its curriculum to include Senior High School strands such as Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM), Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS), and Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM), alongside technical and vocational courses. As a national high school, MFNHS has been central to the community, providing quality education to local students and contributing to the social and economic development of Manolo Fortich.
